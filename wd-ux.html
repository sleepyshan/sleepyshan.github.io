UX/UI Case Study

Project: Design an Ordering Flow for a Florist

Duration:
February 2022 to March 2022

Overview of Product

An e-commerce website to allow customers of a florist to place orders for their products online

Goal
  • The e-commerce website should be designed in a way that allows new products to be highlighted and be in full focus.
  • New products should be given their own separate shopping category to further strengthen the first point.
  • Progress bars should be incorporated into website to allow for a better ordering and post-purchase experience.
Role

For this case study, I took on both the roles of UX Researcher and UX Designer.
This project has gone through the following phases:

  • Primary and Secondary Research
  • User Research
  • Design and Iteration
  • Wireframing
  • Lo-fi and Hi-fi Prototypes
  • Usability Studies

Research: A Summary

I have conducted a survey to find out what kind of challenges people face when placing an order for a flower delivery.
The points users are most concerned with are regarding the status of the delivery once purchase is made, as usually flower orders are really time sensitive. These flowers are usually sent to a same-day special event, and the flowers themselves have a very short shelf life. Users want to be able to update the vendor when unforeseen issues occur, and to also be notified if the florist is also facing unforeseen logistical issues on their end.

High Quality Images

Flowers are highly visual objects so users find it hard to make purchasing decisions when the product image is small and low quality.


Solution:

Large and high quality photographs to be provided of each product.

New Products

As the catalogue changes often, along with the availability of seasonal flowers, users find it hard to distinguish between new products and old ones.


Solution:

New products to be featured front and center in landing page, and given their own shop category.

Transparency

The purchase goes out of their control once payment is made - they donâ€™t know if their purchase has successfully reached the receiver.


Solution:

Multiple progress bars to be included in interface so customer can see what is the delivery status at all times.

Design and Iteration

I started sketching out designs using a method called 'Crazy Eights'.
This was then expanded on and turned into paper wireframes, then digital wireframes, and eventually lo-fi and hi-fi prototypes.
Mockups were also created and merged into the hi-fi prototype.

Accessibility:

A degree of importance has been placed on making the design of the website assessible and user friendly. I have therefore considered these factors while creating the design:

  1. High colour contrast is put into the website, to help to highlight areas where action needs to be taken (buttons, input fields)
  2. Headers are clearly marked as such via font size and other font treatment such as weight and colour
  3. The font sizes used adhere to industry standards and are easy on the eyes when reading

Takeaways

Impact:

The on-demand status updates has allowed customers to check on the status of their deliveries any time they want - and therefore has also translated to less load on the floristâ€™s customer service team.


What I have Learned:

I have learned that improving accessibility in my designs will end up assisting a lot more people, and should keep this in mind moving forward when updating this website in future.
